March 31, 2025

March

Clerical Work

Sir Marc informed us that it would be better to change our schedule—coding days will now be four times a week, with only Mondays dedicated to clerical work. We agreed to this, knowing that we need to finish the system on time. We also learned that this new schedule takes effect starting this week.

So today, we focused on clerical tasks. While some of my co-OJT members scanned documents for surnames starting with the letter L, I pulled out the remaining folders for the same batch. However, we weren’t able to complete the scanning since there was a scheduled meeting in the afternoon for all registrars from various departments and campuses, which we were also required to attend.

The meeting included the introduction of the system we’re working on (BUSAS), which was presented by our mentor, Sir Marc. The presentation went smoothly, and the meeting ended earlier than expected. We even got to enjoy some food and drinks from the meeting.

April 2, 2025

April

Coding

There was a scheduled power interruption today, so we were informed to work from home. I started working at the usual time, 8 am, and we notified our mentor on Discord that we were online.

When working from home, we’re required to ensure that at least one of us pushes our progress to GitHub to confirm we’re working.

My tasks today included fixing the edit functionality to fetch fields that weren’t being retrieved since the feature was recently added. Another task was to fix the signatories page and the receipt modals. I was able to finish all the tasks before 5 pm and successfully pushed the updates to our GitHub repository.
