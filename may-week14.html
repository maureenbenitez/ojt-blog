May 5, 2025

April

Coding

Today was supposed to be our only clerical workday of the week. However, since we had our final presentation at ICTO with Sir Aris—the ICTO Director—and the programmers, we made some adjustments. In the morning, we focused on preparations: fixing remaining errors and organizing the data we planned to present. The presentation went well. Sir Aris and the programmers gave valuable insights and suggestions on how we can improve the system to make it ready for deployment and use.

May 6, 2025

April

Coding

I continued working on my previous task related to the ladderized graduation title. I improved the toggle button functionality, as the data wasn’t being fetched properly on the Edit and View pages. We also started implementing the suggested improvements to the system—specifically, we focused on how to handle data for Regular and Irregular students.

May 7, 2025

April

Coding

The handling of Regular and Irregular students is now fixed, including the creation and viewing features. However, some data is still not being fetched properly in the Edit Students page due to recent code adjustments. I worked on this task and was able to fix the Edit Students.

Another suggestion from the presentation was to make the process of inputting final grades easier. I also worked on this by adding validations—for example, typing 'I' automatically inputs "INC," 'D' for "DRP," and other similar shortcuts.

May 8, 2025

May

Coding

We just realized that we hadn’t included the “Copy For” section for other schools, which is needed for undergraduate students who are transferring. I was tasked with adding input fields on the front end for this—specifically, the name of the school to transfer to and its address. I also added the corresponding columns through a migration to store this data in the database.

May 9, 2025

May

Last Day

We didn’t do much work since it was our last day. However, we still completed the alpha list for the “M” and the first batches of “O” that we had scanned a week ago—just to make sure we wrapped things up properly. After that, a small program was held to mark the completion of our internship. We were congratulated and commended for our contributions during our stay at URO. To celebrate our last day, we shared a meal with the team and also brought food as a token of thanks to our mentors and the URO staff.

Looking back, it was a meaningful experience filled with learning, growth, and great people. I’m grateful for the opportunity and everything I’ve learned during my time at URO.
